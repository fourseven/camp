What is a Rails Camp?

Imagine yourself and a posse of like-minded ruby hackers on a country retreat with zero internet for a weekend of fun. You'll laugh, hack, learn, cry (well, you probably won’t cry… but you know… it felt poetic) and most likely play a crap-load of Werewolf.

Really. No internet! No cell coverage too.

Check out the photo album from the last camp - it's going to be gorgeous scenery and awesome fun.

Things which may or may not happen:

  • Hacking (bring a project idea!)
  • Learning (bring a speaking topic)
  • Werewolf
  • A hike up the hill
  • Drinking & Eating (see below)
  • Meeting cool people
  • General merryment

Where, When, How Much?

We are returning to the now legendary venue of the first South Island camp, Mount Cheeseman Snowline Lodge. Located at 1500m above sea level in the Craigieburn Range the lodge offers a comfortable and breathtaking location for the discerning Rails Camper.

Be at Christchurch Airport by 16:00 on 7th March if you want to catch the bus out to Mt Cheeseman. On the morning of Mon 10th March we'll get you back to the airport at or before 2pm. Don't book your return flight before then!

Tickets are NZ$325. This includes accommodation, 3 meals a day & snacks for the duration of the camp, provided as last time by the team from Country Feasts, a bus there and back from CHCH Airport, a supply of NZ craft beer and cider courtesy of GitHub, soft drinks courtesy of Trineo and espresso coffee courtesy of Resolve Digital.

What to bring?

  • A sleeping bag & pillow
  • A towel
  • Clothes for warm *and* cold weather. March is late summer in NZ and there is the potential for a wide range of daytime temperatures during the camp, and nights may be potentially cold at the high altitude of the venue.
  • Sunscreen.
  • All necessary chargers / power adapters for any electronic equipment you intend to bring.
  • The venue is very isolated, so please ensure you bring any medication you require and additional necessary items with you.
  • There will be NO CELLPHONE RECEPTION at the venue. Really ... none.
    Therefore, please make sure you have all the software and files you need installed. We will be running a mirror of rubygems.org, but to avoid disappointment please assume that you need to be fully self sufficient for any hacking you want to do. For a good rule of thumb, imagine you are MacGyver.
  • You will be sharing dorm / bunkroom style accommodation with other attendees, so bring ear plugs / eye mask if you feel you will need them.
